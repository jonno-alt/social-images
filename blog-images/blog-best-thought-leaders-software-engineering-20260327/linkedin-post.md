I spent weeks researching the 50 best thought leaders in software engineering.

Not just the famous names. Real practitioners, newsletter authors, architecture experts, DevOps pioneers, and engineering leaders who are actively shaping how software gets built in 2026.

Here are three standouts from the list:

1. Gergely Orosz turned The Pragmatic Engineer into the #1 tech newsletter on Substack with 1.1M subscribers. His deep dives on engineering culture and compensation are unmatched.

2. Tanya Reilly wrote The Staff Engineer's Path and her viral talk "Being Glue" sparked a global conversation about invisible work in engineering teams.

3. Charity Majors co-founded Honeycomb and pioneered modern observability. Her blog charity.wtf is essential reading for anyone running production systems.

The full list of 50 covers engineering management, software architecture, DevOps, testing, open source, and more.
