## How to Choose Which Procurement Thought Leaders to Follow

With so many voices in the procurement space, it helps to have a framework for deciding who deserves your attention. Here are seven criteria to consider when curating your own list of procurement thought leaders to follow.

### 1. Relevance to Your Role and Industry

Not every thought leader will speak to your specific challenges. A CPO at a Fortune 500 manufacturer faces different issues than a procurement manager at a mid-sized tech company. Look for leaders whose experience and insights align with your context.

### 2. Active LinkedIn Presence

The best procurement thought leaders are not just famous. They are actively sharing insights, engaging in discussions, and creating content that helps practitioners. Check their posting frequency and the quality of engagement on their posts.

### 3. Depth of Experience

Look for a track record that goes beyond surface-level commentary. The most valuable thought leaders have held senior procurement roles, built companies, written books, or conducted original research that has moved the profession forward.

### 4. Diversity of Perspective

Procurement benefits from diverse viewpoints. Follow leaders from different geographies, industries, backgrounds, and areas of specialisation, whether that is digital transformation, sustainability, supplier innovation, or negotiation.

### 5. Practical Value

The best thought leaders give you something you can use on Monday morning. Favour those who share frameworks, case studies, templates, and actionable advice over those who only deal in abstract theory.

### 6. Community Building

Some thought leaders have built communities, podcasts, newsletters, or platforms that bring practitioners together. These community builders often provide the most sustained value because they create spaces for ongoing learning.

### 7. Forward-Looking Vision

Procurement is changing fast. AI, sustainability mandates, geopolitical disruption, and new operating models are reshaping the function. Follow leaders who are thinking about where procurement is heading, not just where it has been.

## What to Expect When Following Procurement Thought Leaders

Following procurement thought leaders is one of the most effective ways to accelerate your professional development without spending a cent. Here is what you can expect.

Most of the leaders on this list are active on LinkedIn, posting several times per week. You will find a mix of long-form articles, quick tips, industry commentary, and responses to current events affecting procurement and supply chains. Many also host podcasts, write newsletters, or run communities where you can engage more deeply.

The best approach is to start by following 10 to 15 leaders whose focus areas align with your interests, then expand your list over time. Engage with their posts by leaving thoughtful comments, and you will quickly find yourself part of a global conversation about the future of procurement.

## Pricing and Cost Guide for Procurement Thought Leadership Resources

Many procurement thought leaders share their insights for free through LinkedIn, podcasts, and blogs. However, some offer paid resources that can deepen your learning.

Books from authors like Peter Smith, Sigi Osagie, Christian Schuh, and Elouise Epstein typically range from AU$25 to AU$60. Online training platforms like Procurement Tactics and SCMDOJO offer courses ranging from free introductory content to premium programmes costing AU$200 to AU$1,500. Membership communities like Procurious and SIG offer free tiers alongside premium membership options. Conference attendance at events like DPW, the World Procurement Congress, or Coupa Inspire can range from AU$1,500 to AU$5,000 depending on the event and ticket level.

For organisations looking to bring in procurement speakers or consultants, keynote fees for the leaders on this list typically range from AU$5,000 to AU$30,000 depending on the speaker's profile, event location, and engagement format.
