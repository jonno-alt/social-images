## POST COPY (ready to copy/paste)

Every leader has two types of work that light them up, and two that quietly drain them.

Most never figure out which is which.

That is why I put together 21 practical tips for leaders using Patrick Lencioni's Working Genius model.

These are lessons learned from facilitating Working Genius sessions with leadership teams across corporates, schools, and nonprofits.

Here are a few that tend to surprise people:

Tip #8: Label every meeting by "elevation" so people know what altitude of thinking is needed before they walk in.

Tip #13: Stop assigning frustration work to capable people. Capability and energy are not the same thing.

Tip #14: Aim for 60% of each person's time in their genius or competency zone. Track it, even informally.

Tip #21: Bring in a Certified Working Genius Facilitator when you want to go deeper than the assessment alone.
